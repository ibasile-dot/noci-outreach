1The approach

The hook gets you IN; the pain is validated AFTER. You don't need certainty before the meeting — you need a specific, believable reason to be given 20 minutes. The hook is a hypothesis stated as a benefit; discovery then confirms the real pain. So: hook → meeting → discovery → confirmed pain → tailored offer.

Show € they gain vs your price, and always frame the gain as money made or capacity to grow — never as "hours saved."

Your price = 10% of the annual gain (usually split monthly) + a one-off setup fee. They keep 90%. And you can take many clients — the same build, resold, multiplies your income.

Scalability = the headline value, for almost all of them

In southern Italy one employee costs roughly €30–38k/year fully loaded. If your automation avoids even one hire — or handles 30–50% more orders/quotes/patients with the same team — that's €30k+/year of value, every year. You're not selling a tool; you're selling growth capacity.

Run the first call in two halves (Ranbir's note)

If the intro gets you a call (not just a quick favour), do discovery AND the pitch on the same call — you rarely get two. First half: ask the pain + manual-work questions, listen, take notes, DON'T sell. Then pivot — "Posso dirti come lo risolverei?" — and pitch tailored to what you just heard, plugging their real numbers into the calculator and asking what they'd pay. Two touchpoints in one call.

5Pricing — your rule and how to validate it

Your rule: 10% of the gain + setup

Annual fee = 10% of the annual value created (usually split monthly). They keep 90% — an easy yes. Plus a one-off setup fee to build. Growth = more clients with the same reused build. Market data backs this: the same workflow can be worth €3k or €15k depending on the value it creates — exactly the 10% logic.

Market benchmarks

  • Italy SME: setup €2–8k; structured €2–10k/mo; payback 6–14mo
  • Global: projects $1.5–15k; retainers $500–8k/mo
  • Retainer client LTV: $22–33k (base), $49–77k (growth), 11–14 months
  • Freelance n8n/Make: $125–250/hr
  • Dental AI receptionist: €199–499/mo

How to validate pricing this week

  • Lurk/search communities: r/automation, r/n8n, r/AI_Agents, r/Entrepreneur, r/msp, Indie Hackers, n8n/Make forums, AI-automation Skool/Discord groups
  • If there's no clear answer → post the question (draft below)
  • Also post in an industry community (e.g. dentists) to learn what they actually pay for AI reception
  • Always ask "how much would you pay?" in discovery — now you have anchors

Validate the price in the room (better than any forum)

"I do exactly this in my day job. How big a problem is this for you — and what would you pay to have it solved?"

Ask it AFTER they've described the pain. Their number anchors your quote and validates pricing in the field.

Floor: never below ~€300/mo. The retainer must cover AI usage (tokens, voice minutes ~$0.10–0.50/min, SMS) plus your margin.
