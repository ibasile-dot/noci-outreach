1The approach

The hook gets you IN; the pain is validated AFTER. You don't need certainty before the meeting — you need a specific, believable reason to be given 20 minutes. The hook is a hypothesis stated as a benefit; discovery then confirms the real pain. So: hook → meeting → discovery → confirmed pain → tailored offer.

Show € they gain vs your price, and always frame the gain as money made or capacity to grow — never as "hours saved."

Your price = 15% of the annual gain (usually split monthly) + a one-off setup fee. They keep 85%. And you can take many clients — the same build, resold, multiplies your income.

Scalability = the headline value, for almost all of them

In southern Italy one employee costs roughly €30–38k/year fully loaded. If your automation avoids even one hire — or handles 30–50% more orders/quotes/patients with the same team — that's €30k+/year of value, every year. You're not selling a tool; you're selling growth capacity.

3Getting introduced & the first meeting

The chain: someone who knows you → introduces you to their contact → who introduces you to the owner or director. Below: the message your connector can forward, and how to run the first meeting.

The message your connector forwards (to introduce you)

"Mia sorella lavora a Londra e aiuta le aziende a utilizzare l'intelligenza artificiale e le automazioni per ridurre il lavoro ripetitivo e crescere, sia in termini di dimensioni che di fatturato, senza necessariamente dover assumere nuovo personale. In questi giorni sta parlando con alcune aziende qui in zona per capire meglio come lavorano e valutare in che modo il suo approccio potrebbe essere utile. Una delle realtà che le interessa è [nome azienda]. Ti chiedevo se ci fosse la possibilità di organizzare una breve chiamata (anche solo di mezz'ora) con te o con qualcuno che gestisce i processi aziendali o la produttività. È in Italia fino a giovedì, quindi se preferite si potrebbe organizzare anche un incontro di persona."

English version, if the connector is international:

"My sister is based in London and helps companies use AI and automation to cut repetitive work and grow — in both size and revenue — without necessarily hiring new staff. She's currently speaking with a few companies in the area to understand how they work and assess where her approach could help. One of the businesses she's interested in is [company]. Would it be possible to set up a short call (even just half an hour) with you, or with whoever runs your business processes or productivity? She's in Italy until Thursday, so an in-person meeting also works if you prefer."

Fill in [company / person]. Note: it doesn't claim you already know their business — it just opens the door.

The first meeting, in 4 steps

1 · Build rapport (a few minutes). Start human: who introduced you, the area, something genuine. No business yet.

2 · Introduce yourself in one sentence and ask permission to run a few questions:

"Mi chiamo Ilaria, vengo da Londra e costruisco soluzioni di intelligenza artificiale che fanno risparmiare e crescere le aziende. Mi piacerebbe raccontarti di più su di me — ma prima vorrei farti un po' di domande per conoscere te e quello che fate."

3 · Customer development: the goal is to uncover the pain points. Ask the questions below, listen, take notes — don't sell.

4 · "Wow": only now you say what you do and show the tailored solution — the ROI page (below).

Questions — part one (works for any company)

"• Raccontami una giornata tipo: da quando arriva un ordine o una richiesta a quando è evasa — chi ci mette le mani?
• Quali di questi passaggi rifate a mano ogni giorno o settimana?
• Adesso che state crescendo, cosa vi rallenta di più?
• Se domani poteste assumere una persona in più, che lavoro noioso le dareste?
• Quante richieste o contatti ricevete, e quanto ci mettete a rispondere?
• Lavorate con l'estero? Come gestite le lingue?
• Cosa vi fa perdere clienti o tempo più spesso?"
[ COMPANY-SPECIFIC QUESTIONS — prepare before the meeting. Use the "Validate" column in the table. E.g. dentist: "how many calls do you miss a week?" · dairy: "how do reorders arrive and who keys them in?" · exporter: "what share of revenue is export?" ]

After the questions: the tailored page, live

With what you heard, build the page on the spot with the "roi-pitch" skill: show on screen what you can do for them and how much they gain or save (DentiVoice-style), one tailored page. Prepare it in advance with their colours and the data you already know, then finish it there with their answers.

5Pricing — your rule and how to validate it

Your rule: 15% of the gain + setup

Annual fee = 15% of the annual value created (usually split monthly). They keep 85% — an easy yes. Plus a one-off setup fee to build. Growth = more clients with the same reused build. Market data backs this: the same workflow can be worth €3k or €15k depending on the value it creates — exactly the 15% logic.

Market benchmarks

  • Italy SME: setup €2–8k; structured €2–10k/mo; payback 6–14mo
  • Global: projects $1.5–15k; retainers $500–8k/mo
  • Retainer client LTV: $22–33k (base), $49–77k (growth), 11–14 months
  • Freelance n8n/Make: $125–250/hr
  • Dental AI receptionist: €199–499/mo

How to validate pricing this week

  • Lurk/search communities: r/automation, r/n8n, r/AI_Agents, r/Entrepreneur, r/msp, Indie Hackers, n8n/Make forums, AI-automation Skool/Discord groups
  • If there's no clear answer → post the question (draft below)
  • Also post in an industry community (e.g. dentists) to learn what they actually pay for AI reception
  • Always ask "how much would you pay?" in discovery — now you have anchors

Validate the price in the room (better than any forum)

"I do exactly this in my day job. How big a problem is this for you — and what would you pay to have it solved?"

Ask it AFTER they've described the pain. Their number anchors your quote and validates pricing in the field.

Floor: never below ~€300/mo. The retainer must cover AI usage (tokens, voice minutes ~$0.10–0.50/min, SMS) plus your margin.
